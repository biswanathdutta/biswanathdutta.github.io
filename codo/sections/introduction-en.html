Introduction back to ToC

In this document, we describe the various classes, object properties, and data properties of CODO. It is primarily to represent and reason information related to COVID-19 cases and patient information. The cases, such as active, recovered, deceased, migrated, etc. on a daily basis across the geo-location (district, state (province), and country), available resources and requirements. The patient and related information, such as nationality, symptom, suspected level of the disease, travel history, inter-personal relationships between patients, suspected transmission reason, tracking of patient test results, diagnosis, treatment facility, clinical facility, etc.

The ontology consists of a total of 44 classes, 51 object properties, and 45 data properties. The ontology has been produced by evaluating various COVID-19 resources, such as datasets, literature, services, etc. CODO defines 87 properties to serve as (i) an explicit ontology to be used by the data and service providers to publish COVID-19 data as a knowledge graph, to develop and offer semantic services and applications (e.g., decision support system, advanced analytics); or (ii) vocabulary to be used by the agencies (e.g., Govt. agencies, hospitals, data publishers, news agencies, etc.) to annotate and describe COVID-19 information.
